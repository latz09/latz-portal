01 A Note From Jordan
Allison & Mary,

Right now, your checkout is costing you customers. Someone places an order, the screen never confirms it, so they place it again — and then they have to call in to cancel the duplicate. That's not a glitch to shrug off. That's a customer's first experience of Honestly Cranberry, and it's been going wrong for a year and a half. "A nightmare," in Mary's words. I agree.

Meanwhile, you're sitting on a story most food brands would give anything for — a 150-year, four-generation Wisconsin cranberry farm, an episode on Wisconsin Foodie, chefs cooking with your fruit, and real published research standing behind your health claims. None of it is doing any work on a site that can't reliably take an order.

So the job is two things at once: move the commerce onto infrastructure that doesn't drop orders, and finally build a storefront that tells the story at the level the brand has earned. The story deserves better, and the checkout has to stop costing you.

The wireframe I sent alongside this shows you exactly how that looks, page by page. This proposal covers the rest — what's in scope, what it costs, and how we start. Custom build, no monthly fees to me, and you own every line of it. Forever.

03 Why This Site Matters

Three reasons this is worth doing — in order of what it does for the bottom line.

  • 01 Margin recovery.

    Every order that moves from a marketplace to your own site keeps the cut that marketplace would otherwise take — often 15–30%. On a single-ingredient food product with thin margins, a site that actually converts isn't a cost center. It's a direct lever on what you keep per order. That's the real return on this build.

  • 02 Reliability you can stop worrying about.

    Shopify processes billions of dollars in orders a year. The duplicate-order problem — the one that's had customers calling in to cancel — disappears. This isn't a tech upgrade for its own sake. It's whether a first-time buyer trusts you enough to come back.

  • 03 The story, finally surfaced.

    Four generations, an episode on Wisconsin Foodie, chefs using your fruit, and published research behind your gut-health claims. Today that's invisible. Pulled out of the footer and put to work, it's the reason a health-conscious buyer picks you over a sweetened, oiled bag from someone with no farm and no story.
