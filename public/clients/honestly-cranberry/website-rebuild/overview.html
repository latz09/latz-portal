Notes & Observations

Post-Discovery — June 5, 2026

Strongest signal: conversation focused on logistics, not convincing. They aren't shopping. They're asking how, not whether. Sole vendor + warm referral + ASAP timeline = strong pricing position. Anchor with confidence at $6,500–$7,500. Don't underprice because they said ASAP — urgency is leverage for Jordan, not them.

Relationship Dynamics

Mary = matriarch + decision-maker (4th gen, the brand is hers). Allison = bridge + day-to-day operator (5th gen, has Emmy/3yo). Pookie = marketing strategist who referred — stay out of her lane on marketing strategy. Don't talk past Mary to Allison. Confirm Mary's review-loop involvement before sending proposal.

Parent-to-Parent Rapport

Allison has 3yo Emmy; Jordan has 3 and 6yo. Kindergarten-pickup constraint surfaced naturally in scheduling. This rapport already exists — don't manufacture it but lean into the working-parent solidarity when appropriate. Family-owned business respects family.

Stack-Framing Discipline

Mary specifically named GoDaddy + WordPress + WooCommerce as a "nightmare." Frame the combo as the problem, NOT WordPress as a category. Mary may bristle at platform-tribal trash talk — she's been on WP for 1.5 years and chose it once. The problem is the stack mix for a non-technical food brand, not WP itself.

Reliability is the Emotional Hook

The duplicate-order-then-cancel story is the moment to come back to repeatedly. That's not a tech problem — that's a customer losing trust in their brand because of a broken checkout. Shopify handles this. Make this concrete in the proposal: "Shopify processes billions in GMV. The duplicate-order problem disappears."

Reviews Strategy — Be Honest

~1,000 Yotpo reviews are a real asset. Don't drop Yotpo. But have the gentle conversation: Yotpo = product-level (huge for conversion); Google = business-level trust. Keep both running, don't try to consolidate everything. Amazon reviews CANNOT be imported (TOS violation) — manage that expectation early so it's not a launch-day disappointment.

Playground Project (Allison Side)

Pookie is also working with Allison on a separate indoor playground project (separate overview). Do NOT bring it up unless Allison does. Mary may not be looped in on it and it could create awkward dynamics in the family-business decision-making.

Pricing Anchor Discipline

Mary's mental anchor is the $2K–$5K Shopify Expert tier — her opening question made that clear ("is this something we can do ourselves… or where do you come in?"). Real US market for what's actually being built is $8K–$20K. Jordan's $6,500–$7,500 is the honest middle. The proposal should reference the real market quietly (without name-checking competitors) so the price feels reasonable rather than premium.

Photography is the One Real Risk

The whole "story-forward homepage" pitch depends on imagery. If they have no usable farm/family photos and won't fund a shoot, the design has to lean harder on text + Wisconsin Foodie video + iconography. Get clarity before proposal lands — don't promise visual storytelling we can't deliver.

"Old GoDaddy Site Was Simpler" Signal

Worth taking seriously. They explicitly liked the previous GoDaddy Sites version (honestlycranberry.godaddysites.com) for its simplicity and cleanness. That's the design north star — restraint over feature density. Save screenshots. Resist the urge to over-design just because we can.
