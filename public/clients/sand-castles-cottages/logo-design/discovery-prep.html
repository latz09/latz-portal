Remaining Questions

🎯
Trimmed down — she already answered a lot Timeline, colors, and usage are covered. What's left is the stuff you need to scope the work and brief Alyssa: property status, what makes it special, design taste, decision-makers, budget, and coordination with Pookie. You can get through these in a 10-minute call — then tell her you'll come back with a project outline.
The Business & Brand
Where are you at with the property — is it being built, renovated, or already running?
Affects whether there are real photos to work from and how the brand will roll out. If it's already taking guests, the logo has immediate use. If it's under construction, there's more runway.
What makes Sand Castles Cottages different from other places to stay up there?
The satellite mini homes concept is the obvious differentiator, but let her describe it. Her words will tell you the brand personality better than the Pinterest board can. This is what Alyssa needs to capture — not just "beach rental."
Any words or feelings you want people to get from the brand?
Not taglines — emotional territory. "Relaxed," "family," "escape," "cozy but upscale" — whatever she says shapes the logo's personality. The taffy giveaway already tells you this brand has a playful, generous side.
Design Taste
Any logos you've seen — from hotels, Airbnbs, restaurants, whatever — that you love the look of?
Interior designer = strong visual opinions. Getting a few "I love that" examples saves revision rounds. Also ask if there's anything she definitely doesn't want — overly corporate, too clipart-y, etc.
Scope & Practical
Is it just you making the call on the logo, or will your husband want to weigh in too?
Critical for the concept presentation stage. If he's involved, both need to see concepts at the same time. Don't design for one person's taste and get overruled.
Do you have a budget range in mind?
Frame it naturally: "That helps me figure out how many concepts to include and what deliverables make sense." If she doesn't have a number, you'll give her a range in the project outline after you understand the full scope.
Coordination
What's Pookie working on for you right now on the brand side?
Pookie is doing brand voice. You need to know what she's delivering and roughly when, so the logo and the voice work don't happen in separate vacuums. If Pookie has brand words or a direction doc, that's useful input for Alyssa.
Website — is that something you're thinking about soon or more down the road?
Affects how you price and position the logo. If the website is coming in the next couple months, you can mention it's something you handle and plant the seed without pushing.

Callback Script

📱
This is a quick call, not a formal meeting She left a voicemail, you're calling back. Keep it casual and conversational — she's already given you a bunch. You've got maybe 6–8 questions left. Goal: get what you need to scope the work, then set up an in-person meeting. Tell her you'll come back with a project outline for the logo.
Open Hey Jen, it's Jordan from Latz Web Design — calling you back. Thanks for that voicemail, that was super helpful actually. You covered a lot of what I was going to ask.
Acknowledge what she told you So I've got the timeline — no huge rush, which is great. Blues, whites, natural wood, wicker for the palette direction. And the logo going on signs, shirts, stickers — including the small ones for the taffy, which I love by the way. That's such a good touch being right by Mackinac.
This shows you actually listened to her voicemail. She'll appreciate that.
Transition — just a few more things I just have a handful of things I want to make sure I understand before I put together a project outline for you. Quick stuff.
Question 1 — Property status Where are you at with the property right now — is it being built, renovated, or are you already up and running?
This tells you if there are real photos, how soon the logo is actually needed, and how tangible the project is.
Question 2 — What makes it special Tell me about the setup with the mini homes — Pookie mentioned a main house with satellite cottages around it. What's the vision there?
Let her talk. Her words about her own property are gold for the design brief. This is what makes it different from every other UP Airbnb.
Question 3 — Brand feel When people see the brand — the logo, the name, whatever — what do you want them to feel? Like, what's the vibe you're going for?
She'll probably say something like "relaxed" or "family-friendly but nice." Whatever she says, write it down — Alyssa needs this.
Question 4 — Design taste Are there any logos you've seen — doesn't have to be hotels, just anything — where you thought "I love that"? Or anything you definitely don't want?
Interior designer — she has opinions. This saves revision cycles. Even a "I don't want anything too corporate" is useful.
Question 5 — Decision-makers And on the logo — is it just you deciding, or will your husband want to weigh in on that too?
Keep it light. You're not interrogating — just need to know who's in the room when you present concepts.
Question 6 — Budget Do you have a budget range in mind? That helps me figure out how many concepts to include and what makes sense for deliverables.
If she doesn't have a number, that's fine — tell her you'll include pricing in the project outline. Don't push.
Question 7 — Pookie coordination What's Pookie working on for you right now? I just want to make sure the logo and whatever she's doing on brand voice are in sync.
Quick check. If Pookie has brand words or a direction doc already, ask Jen if she can share it.
Set up in-person This is all really helpful. I'd love to meet up in person before we kick things off — even just a quick coffee. It's way easier to get on the same page face to face. What works for you this week or next?
In-person builds the relationship. She's outgoing and articulate — this will be a good meeting. Lock down a day and place.
Close — project outline Cool. So here's what I'll do — I'll put together a project outline for the logo based on everything we've talked about. That'll cover what's included, how many concepts you'll see, the revision process, timeline, pricing — all of it. I'll have that for you before we meet so you can look it over.
"Project outline" — not proposal. It's friendlier, less formal, and matches the tone of this relationship. She'll get the scope, the process, and the price in one clean doc.
Sign off Really looking forward to this one, Jen. Talk soon.
