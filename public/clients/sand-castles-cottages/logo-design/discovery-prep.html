Discovery Questions

🎯
Notes to self — not a script These are the things you need to understand before you can scope this, price it, and brief Alyssa. You already know the aesthetic direction and the business concept. Fill in the gaps — don't re-ask what Pookie already told you.
The Business & Brand
Tell me more about the property — where are you in the build?
Need to know if this is under construction, near completion, or already operating. Affects timeline pressure and whether there are photos of the actual property to work with.
What sets Sand Castles Cottages apart from other Airbnbs in the UP?
The satellite mini homes concept is unusual. Understanding what makes this special helps Alyssa design a logo that captures the right identity — not just "another beach rental."
Have you committed to any colors for the property interiors or exteriors yet?
The Pinterest board is heavy on blues, whites, and natural wood. If she's already choosing paint colors, siding, or interior finishes for the actual property, the logo should live in that same palette. Interior designer — she's probably already deep into material selections.
Any words or feelings you want people to get when they see the brand?
Not about taglines — about emotional associations. "Relaxed luxury," "family escape," "private beach getaway" — these shape the design direction.
Design Preferences & Usage
Where will this logo show up most?
Airbnb listing, social media, signage at the property, printed materials, towels/merch? This affects whether Alyssa needs to design for small screens, large-format printing, embroidery, or all of the above. Might need a simplified icon version alongside a full logo.
Any logos from other businesses — hospitality or otherwise — that you love?
Jennifer probably has strong visual taste given her interior design background. Knowing what she gravitates toward (and what she hates) saves revision cycles.
Are there any existing brand elements we need to work with or around?
Any colors already chosen for the property interiors, signage already ordered, social media accounts already live with placeholder branding, etc.
Scope & Practical
What's the timeline look like — is there a date this needs to be done by?
Seasonal business in the UP. If they're trying to launch for summer 2026, that changes urgency. If the property is still being built, there may be more breathing room.
Have you had a logo designed before or is this your first time through the process?
Sets expectations for how you explain the process — concepts, revisions, final delivery. Interior design background means she understands creative process, but logo design specifically might be new territory.
Is it just you making the call on the logo, or will your husband weigh in too?
Need to know who the decision-makers are before presenting concepts. Nothing kills a project faster than designing for one person's taste and then finding out someone else has final say.
Do you have a budget range in mind for the logo?
Frame it as helping you scope the right level of work — not as a gotcha. "That helps me figure out how many concepts to include and what makes sense for deliverables." If she doesn't have a number, give her a range after you understand scope.
Looking Ahead
Pookie mentioned a website will probably follow — is that something you're thinking about now or down the road?
If the website is imminent, that affects how Alyssa approaches the logo — it needs to work as a web header, favicon, etc. Also affects how you price the logo if there's a bundled website opportunity coming.
Anything else Pookie is handling on the brand side that I should know about?
Pookie is developing brand voice. Make sure you know what she's delivering and when, so the logo and the voice work are aligned — not designed in separate vacuums.
