02 What I Heard
Everything below is based on our call and the info you've shared so far. If anything's off or missing, hit "Add a note" and let me know — even small corrections help.
Your Business
NameSand Castle Cottages
What It IsVacation rental property — a main house with satellite mini cottage homes around it for guests and family. On the beach in Michigan's Upper Peninsula, near Mackinac Island.
AudienceDual audience — families building beach memories and couples looking for a romantic getaway. Not exclusively one or the other.
BookingGuests find you through Vrbo, Booking.com, and Airbnb. The logo is what makes your listing feel like a destination, not just another rental.
ExperienceYou and Jeff ran a previous Airbnb called "Longview" — you know the business. This isn't your first time building a brand around a rental property.
Fun DetailNear Mackinac Island, famous for fudge and taffy. You're planning to give away taffy to guests with branded stickers on the bags.
The Vibe
OverallUpscale coastal cottage. Elevated, high-end, but warm and approachable. Think Nantucket or Cape Cod — not a beach bar.
Art StyleWatercolor / painting feel — you mentioned this multiple times. Not photographic, not clipart. Organic, hand-done, artful.
Vibe WordsBeachy, cozy, family-friendly, upscale, grown-up, relaxed
What It's NotNot childish, not generic beach clipart. Grown-up and refined, but still fun and inviting.
Color Direction — approximate, not final
These are rough swatches based on what we discussed — just to make sure we're in the same ballpark. The designer will refine the exact palette once we start on concepts.
Dominant
White — lots of it. Clean, airy.
Primary
Soft, refined light blue — not sea glass, not blue-green
Accent
Navy — touch of it, also needed darker for road-visible signage
Accent 2

AvoidSea glass blue-green, chartreuse (was your previous cottage), overly bright or primary colors
Logo Ideas & Elements
SandcastlePotentially — as a watercolor, abstract, or subtle element. Something that nods to the name without being the whole logo.
DirectionI know you and Jeff have been exploring different ideas for what the logo could include. We'll present a few distinct concepts so you both have real options to react to.
InitialsYou mentioned playing with a bigger S and two smaller C's. Worth exploring as an icon mark.
BackgroundWatercolor wash of Lake Michigan and sand, or a sketch of what the house will look like — you mentioned both as possibilities.
StructureYou liked the layout and structure of a lighthouse-style logo you shared — not the lighthouse itself, but how it was composed.
SignageLogo needs to be readable from the road. The name might need to be in a darker color for visibility on signage.
Inspiration
PinterestYour Pinterest Board ↗ — coastal cottage, white shiplap, navy textiles, natural wood beams, woven pendants, gray-blue cedar shingles, covered porches
ScreenshotYou sent one logo screenshot you liked. Said you'd send more — any additional examples help us nail the direction faster.
Previous Brand"Longview" — your previous Airbnb. Navy blue and chartreuse. You loved those colors at the time but you're moving in a different direction for this one — coral as the warm accent instead.
Where It'll Live
Signage1–2 road signs, visible from the road. This is the primary large-format use.
Social MediaProfile picture and cover images — Facebook confirmed, likely others. Needs a compact icon mark that works at tiny sizes.
MerchT-shirts, sweatshirts. Jeff already has a merch vendor.
StickersRegular stickers and small ones for the taffy giveaway bags. Needs to work at a small scale.
Future WebsiteNot in this project, but the typography and color system from the logo work will carry directly into a future site build.
