01 A Note From Jordan
Jen,

Sand Castle Cottages is the kind of brand that needs to feel right the moment someone sees it. You're building a property that guests will find through Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com — platforms where every listing looks the same. The logo is what makes yours feel like a place, not just a listing. It's the thing that shows up on your road sign, your social profiles, the stickers on those taffy bags, the sweatshirts guests take home. It sets the tone before they ever set foot on the property.

From our conversation, I know exactly the direction this needs to go — upscale coastal, watercolor feel, whites and soft blues and coral, the kind of brand that appeals to couples on a weekend away and families building memories. Not childish, not generic beach clipart. Elevated but warm. You have strong visual instincts from your interior design work, and I want to make sure the logo lives up to what you're building.

I've put together three options below — a focused logo package that covers the essentials, a full brand identity with guidelines and social assets, and a complete brand toolkit that locks in the entire visual system across every touchpoint — listings, signage, print, merch, and marketing materials.

03 Why This Matters

When someone scrolls through vacation rentals, they're making snap decisions. They look at the first photo, they skim the listing title, and they glance at the host profile. Your logo is the one visual that separates Sand Castle Cottages from the hundreds of other beach rentals in Northern Michigan. It's the difference between "this feels like a real destination" and "this is someone's second house on Airbnb."

You're not competing with hotels — you're competing with every other listing in the Straits area. A strong brand turns a vacation rental into a place people remember, recommend, and come back to. It's the thing that makes guests pick up a taffy bag and say "this place has it together." It's the thing that makes your road sign feel like an arrival, not just a turn.

The logo isn't decoration — it's the first promise you make to every guest. And with the property, the interiors, and the experience you're planning, that promise needs to match.
