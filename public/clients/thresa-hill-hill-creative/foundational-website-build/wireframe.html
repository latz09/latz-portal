What to Expect — 4-Step Process
01
Book Online
Schedule your shoot through my easy online booking. I'll confirm your date and send a prep guide to get the property ready.
02
The Shoot
I arrive on time, ready to work efficiently. Most shoots take 1–2 hours depending on property size. You can be present or hand off the key.
03
24-Hour Delivery
Edited, high-resolution images delivered within 24 hours via a private online gallery — ready for your MLS listing.
04
Use & Share
Download, share, and use your images across MLS, social media, and marketing materials. Virtual tours are hosted and ready to link.
All 4-step copy from Thresa's AI mockup — she already articulated this process clearly. The 24-hour turnaround is the key selling point. Agents want speed and predictability.
What Happens on Shoot Day
When I arrive for your photoshoot, the home should be photo-ready, but I'll still help with the final touches — I'll turn on all the lights, put down toilet lids, and tidy small things like countertop clutter. The rest is up to you, so check the provided checklist beforehand.
Feel free to head out for coffee and leave me entry instructions, or hang out on another floor while I work. I'll go through the home three times — once for photos, once for video, and once for a 360° tour if you've signed up for it. Depending on size and services, this can take 45 minutes to 2 hours — great time to grab a coffee with your clients.
We don't usually photograph closets or cupboards unless they're a feature, so they're perfect for hiding clutter. Need something changed? Just shoot me a message — I can remove objects, virtually stage, or add twilight images in post.
This detail copy is from Thresa's current live GoDaddy site — it's great, practical stuff that agents love. The casual tone (toilet lids, coffee break, hiding clutter in closets) is very on-brand. Jordan will tighten for the new design.

✓ Confirmed Decisions
  • ✅
    6-Page Phase 1 Scope — Home, Real Estate, Branding, Portraits, About, Contact. No client portal, no blog, no payment system in this build.
  • ✅
    Embed Existing Tools — Don't Replace Them — Calendly stays for RE scheduling. HoneyBook stays for CRM/inquiry. Pic-Time stays off-site for gallery delivery. Phase 1 is a front door, not a platform swap.
  • ✅
    CMS in Phase 1 — Sanity CMS included from the start. Thresa manages portfolio images, testimonials, and basic page content herself. No need to call Jordan for routine updates like swapping photos or adding testimonials.
  • ✅
    Three Audience Routing — Homepage routes RE agents (speed → Calendly), branding clients (premium → HoneyBook), and family/portrait clients (warm → HoneyBook). Each path has its own service page.
  • ✅
    "Don't Feel Sold To" Principle — No Book Now buttons in the nav. CTAs live on the pages in context, after the content has done its job. Nav feels like a table of contents.
  • ✅
    Design Direction — Stonebridge Marketing is the vibe reference. AI mockup (Cormorant Garamond + Jost, rose/sage/teal) is the starting point. Editorial, calm, confident.
