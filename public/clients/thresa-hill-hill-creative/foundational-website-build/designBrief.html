Project Snapshot

The job

Hill Creative is Thresa — a real person with a real story, not a faceless studio. The site has to route three very different audiences through one calm, editorial front door without ever feeling like a sales funnel. The guiding principle, in her words: visitors should feel like they've already decided and are just here to figure out the next steps. The work should speak; the site should get out of its way.

The Build
Scope6-page site, two-stage build — one design job with a natural break in the middle
Stage 1 (now)Homepage, About, Contact — where the design system gets established
CMSSanity — portfolio, testimonials, service content, About
StackNext.js, React, Tailwind, Framer Motion, Vercel
Not in Scope
Portal / PayNo client portal, no payment system
Booking UICalendly & HoneyBook embedded as-is — no custom booking interface
ContentNo blog
OtherNo mobile app, no dark mode.
🧭
Where your energy goes — Stage 1 is the real design work Stage 1 establishes everything: type rules, color application, component patterns, spacing, the editorial feel, how photography is presented, how CTAs land without pushing. The homepage alone carries three service sections with carousels, testimonials, and an about blurb. Stage 2 is applying that system, not redesigning — you'll design one service-page layout that gets reused three times, plus a toggle on Contact. Front-load the creative heavy lifting in Stage 1 and Stage 2 comes together fast.

Let's Talk

☕
Bring your instincts — not just answers These are the things worth talking through before we're deep in build mode. You know editorial design and photography presentation better than I do — if you've got a direction you want to run with, let's hear it. Conversation starters, not a checklist. We're presenting Thresa two directions to choose from, so come with opinions.
Open Design Questions
Three service types on one homepage — without it becoming a catalog or a funnel
Each section needs enough weight to stand alone in Stage 1 (the dedicated pages don't exist yet) but not so much that the page feels endless. How do we give each service room to breathe and still keep the page calm and editorial?
The swipeable carousels — how should they feel?
Arrow buttons on desktop, swipe on mobile. Dots or a counter? The challenge is making them sit inside the editorial aesthetic rather than reading as a generic slider component. What's the most restrained, intentional version of this?
Hero treatment — is the staggered-photo move the right one?
Her AI mockup used overlapping/staggered photos at different sizes. Is that the strongest approach for a photography site, or does the work speak louder with something cleaner? Curious where your eye goes.
The "Now Featuring" brand photography section — curated, not just another section
How do we make this feel current and editorial — like a featured spread — rather than one more block on the page? This is the moment that can signal "she's working, she's relevant" if it's handled right.
Testimonials that feel editorial, not template-y
One strong quote on the homepage, more on About. How do these read like a magazine pull-quote rather than a starred review widget? Typography and placement probably do most of the work here.
Elevating beyond the template Stonebridge is built on
Stonebridge runs a Davey & Krista Elementor template — Thresa loves the vibe, but we're building custom. Where do we push past what a template can do? That gap is the whole reason she's hiring us instead of buying a theme.
