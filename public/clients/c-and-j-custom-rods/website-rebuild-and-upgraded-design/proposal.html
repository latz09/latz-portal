01 A Note From Jordan
Chris,

When I built the first C&J site, you were selling custom rods out of a passion project with no real commerce engine behind it. A year later you're carrying reels, launching apparel, investing in professional photography, and outgrowing every part of that original build. That's not a website problem — that's a growth problem. And it's a good one to have.

The current site did its job. But it was never built for what C&J is becoming. Orders are fully manual, new product categories require developer work every time, and the design doesn't reflect the quality of what you're putting out now. You need a storefront that runs like an actual business — where you manage products, process orders, and print shipping labels yourself, without calling me.

That's what this rebuild is. Same CMS you already know for content, but with a real commerce engine behind it — Shopify handling orders, payments, inventory, and shipping. The custom rod builder rebuilt smarter. A design that matches the new photography. And a site you can actually run day-to-day on your own.

This proposal covers the full 8-page ground-up rebuild — everything we talked about at Point Brewery. I've also put together a complete wireframe showing every page, every section, and how it all fits together. Take a look at both and let me know when you're ready to go.

03 Why This Rebuild Matters

The current site was built for a different version of your business. Orders go through manually — no dashboard, no shipping labels, no inventory tracking. Adding a new product category means calling a developer. The design was built before professional photography, before reels, before apparel.

You're running a growing business on a site that can't keep up. Every order you process manually is time you're not spending building rods or growing the brand. Every customer who can't check out with Apple Pay or see real-time inventory is friction you don't need.

This rebuild puts a real commerce engine behind the brand — and gives you a storefront that actually matches the quality of what you're making.
