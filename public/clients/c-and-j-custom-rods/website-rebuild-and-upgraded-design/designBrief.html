Project Snapshot

The job

Ground-up rebuild of Chris's custom fishing rod business — moving off a year-old Next.js + Sanity storefront with zero native commerce onto Sanity for content plus a headless Shopify backend for orders, inventory, and checkout. The custom rod builder gets rebuilt from scratch and stays fully custom since Shopify's variant system can't handle it. Chris is also adding reels and apparel as new categories, with professional photography rolling in across two shoots. This is a returning client and a portfolio piece — make it look like current-year work, not a patch job.

The Build
Scope8-page custom rebuild — Home, Shop (Rods/Reels/Apparel), Custom Rod Builder, About, Testimonials, Contact
CMSSanity — pages, copy, testimonials. Same system Chris already knows.
CommerceShopify Storefront API — products, inventory, checkout, payments, shipping
StackNext.js 15 (App Router), React, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion, Vercel
Timeline~6–8 weeks, phased around the second photography shoot
Not in Scope (Future Upsells)
Email MarketingShopify's built-in flows — abandoned cart, post-purchase — future add-on
Blog / ContentFishing tips, rod-building process, care guides — future SEO play
Advanced ShippingMulti-carrier / automated rates — only if order volume demands it
Maintenance RetainerOngoing updates after launch — separate future conversation

Let's Talk

☕
Bring your instincts — not just answers These are things worth talking through before we're deep in build mode. You know design better than I do — if you've got a direction you want to run with, let's hear it. These are conversation starters, not a checklist.
Open Design Questions
How literally should we carry the existing green/blue forward?
There's real brand equity in the current mark. Does the palette carry through as primary colors, shrink to an accent against something new, or split the difference? Would love to see a couple directions.
Typography that feels rugged and premium at once
This is a handmade product commanding a real price. Needs to feel crafted and trustworthy, not novelty-outdoors and not corporate-sterile. What pairs well with a strong display face here?
How does the hero hold up before photography lands?
Batch 1 is already in hand (landing in the portal), but batch 2 isn't shot yet. What's the fallback treatment — color block, illustrated texture, pattern — so later pages don't stall waiting on the second round?
Visual weight of the custom rod builder
This page carries the highest-ticket purchase on the site. How much personality vs. clean, confidence-inspiring utility should it have? It needs to feel precise, not fiddly.
Should rods, reels, and apparel feel like one voice or three?
Reels and apparel are brand new categories for Chris. Worth deciding now whether they read as extensions of the custom rod craftsmanship story or their own lighter retail sections.
