Notes & Observations

May 27, 2026 — Deal closed in 7 days from first contact

May 20 referral → May 27 paid deposit. That's a 7-day sales cycle for a $3,500 custom build. Three factors made this work: (1) the personalized audit page sent before the meeting did 80% of the selling, (2) showing up at the showroom in person turned a transaction into a relationship, (3) responding to Jeff's "two employees" credibility concern in the morning by adding the "How We're Built" section to the proposal the same day. The pattern to remember: pre-sell with concrete artifacts, show up in person when possible, listen for what they say and visibly incorporate it within hours.

Relationship Context

Jeff is 63, 22 years in the business, no committee. Direct decision-maker. Prefers text. Used the phrase "win-win" unprompted at the showroom — he sees this as a partnership, not a transaction. Megan Melum is the referral source — protect that channel. First Megan conversion · she'll send more if this lands well.

Risks

Scope creep risk now elevated. Phase 1 was tightly scoped to make $3,500 honest. Jeff has CMS access promised, which solves most "can we add..." requests. But during build he will likely surface ideas — e-commerce for furniture, business opportunities page, embedded 3D walkaround, calculator rebuild. Hold the line. These are Phase 2/3. Reference the wireframe and the proposal's explicit "what's not included" framing when they come up.

Communication Style

Text first. Phone second. Email for documents and links. Jeff doesn't want a portal, a project management tool, or weekly status calls — he wants the dev link, the questions when they come up, and the launch date. Match his rhythm. Send fewer, denser updates.

Wireframe + Proposal Reference

Canonical scope docs live at latzwebdesign.com/clients/classic-log-homes/website-rebuild/wireframe.html and /proposal.html. When scope drift surfaces during the build, those docs are the boundary. The proposal's "what's not included" section was written for this exact moment.

90-Day Success Metric

The deal that justifies Phase 1 is the first log package sale attributable to the new site within 90 days of launch. Frame the post-launch check-in around that. If it happens, Phase 2 sells itself. If it doesn't, the conversation is about iteration — not refund.
