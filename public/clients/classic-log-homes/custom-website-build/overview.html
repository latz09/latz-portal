Use the word "win-win" naturally and often. It's his filter for every decision. Mirror his "supplier not builder" framing constantly. Don't lead with Golden Eagle comparison — he's reframed: he's NOT competing with them, different product entirely.

The Richard Situation — Handle With Care

Previous developer Richard was a church friend. Jeff fired him for not being win-win — nickel-and-diming, charging for phone calls, slow on tiny edits. The fallout still hurts. DON'T pile on Richard. Don't dunk on his work. The CMS self-edit capability is Jordan's most valuable feature to Jeff specifically because of this — it's the direct antidote to Richard trauma. The Sale Was Already Made — Don't Screw It Up

The audit page pre-sold him on the engagement. The phone call confirmed it. The May 25 meeting was about not screwing it up. The proposal is about giving him something safe to say yes to. Win-win paragraph is the heart of the proposal. Lead with what's INCLUDED, then transparently list what's NOT and why — this builds trust by showing scope discipline rather than hiding cuts.

CMS Confidence Note

Jeff already edits his own LinkedIn, Google Business, and Industry Net listings. He WILL use the CMS heavily. Spend the design time making it dead-simple — pre-fillable defaults on "what's included" repeaters, clear field labels, no jargon. This is the relationship retention play.

Scope creep risk: Tommy (friend/advisor) may continue to push "simpler / cheaper" mid-build. Protect scope discipline. Don't let scope shrink further once approved — but also don't expand.

Timeline risk: Aug 1, 2026 deadline is firm-ish. If Jeff drags on floor plan selection or content delivery, launch slips. Old site won't go dark, but parts decay. Set clear content deadlines at kickoff.

Framing risk: "Family-owned" may need revisiting if Jeff feels it's inaccurate (just him + wife). Confirm at kickoff.

Value Framing for Internal Reference

Phase 1 is scoped to what gets Jeff to his next sale — nothing more. $0/month operating cost forever after Phase 1 (no plugin renewals, no maintenance retainer, no "license expired" emails). 3-year cost of ownership vs typical agency maintenance: $5,000–$8,000+ saved.
