03 Why This Site Matters

There are a few problems stacking up — and the new site fixes all of them at once.

Your site is talking to the wrong people.

Visitors assume you're a turnkey builder. Owner-builders and contractors — your actual audience — never see themselves on the page. The new site opens with "we supply the package, you build the home" and filters correctly in the first five seconds.

You can't update your own site.

Every change required a developer, cost money, and took weeks. The new site is built on a custom CMS where you edit prices, add floor plans, swap photos, and upload PDFs yourself — instantly, no phone calls, no charges, ever.

You've been paying for things you didn't choose.

Plugin licenses, maintenance retainers, hosting fees. The new site has no licenses to renew, no monthly fee to me for the first 12 months, and $0 ongoing operating cost going forward.

The site makes you look smaller than you are.

No story about your Wisconsin sawmill partners, the manufacturing network behind every package, or the 22 years of relationships behind the materials you ship. The new site puts that front and center — especially for builders who need to know there's real capacity behind the supplier they're calling.

And the clock is running.

After August 1, plugin licenses on the old site start expiring and the custom calculator becomes unmaintainable. You need a clean path forward now — one you fully own, with no ongoing fees, that grows with the business.
