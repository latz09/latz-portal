Notes & Observations

Rhomb Research Findings

Storefronts live at {businessname}.rhombnow.com with deep-linkable product URLs — confirmed by real examples (playdatemn.rhombnow.com, littleplaydate.rhombnow.com). White-labeled storefronts with logo + business name. Party flow handled entirely by Rhomb (host books, gets a shareable waiver link, each guest gets their own code). Built-in email automations, promo codes, gift cards, native payment processing — no Stripe/Square integration needed, no newsletter platform needed. Rhomb positions itself as complementary to a marketing site, not a replacement. Pricing $199–$229/mo for the client. Setup takes ~5–6 weeks (2–3 weeks storefront + 3 weeks product config). Two questions still out to sales: storefront analytics support + visual customization depth.

Rhomb Parallel Setup — Timeline Critical

Allison and Alex must start Rhomb configuration in parallel with the web build, not after. Rhomb's ~5–6 week setup window means if they wait until the marketing site is done to begin, the February launch slips. Flag this clearly in the proposal and again at kickoff. Jordan also needs the final Rhomb product URLs before launch to wire up deep-link CTAs.

Deep-Link UX as a Differentiator

Most template builds dump users on a generic "Book Now" storefront homepage. The plan here is to deep-link each CTA — "Buy a Day Pass," "Get a Yearly Membership," "Book Your Party" — directly to the specific Rhomb product URL, opening in a new tab so the marketing site stays open behind the booking. Small detail, real UX impact, and a concrete "we sweat the details" talking point in the proposal vs. competitors on Wix.
