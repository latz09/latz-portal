01

Project Snapshot

What this site needs to do

This is an unstaffed venue — no front desk, no one greeting people at the door. The website carries 100% of the pre-visit burden: it has to earn a parent's trust before they'll book a self-service play space for their kid, then hand off cleanly to Rhomb for every transaction. Nothing else needs to happen here.

The Build

Scope 2 pages, both CMS-managed — Home (About + FAQ) and Offerings
Stack Next.js / React / Tailwind, custom CMS, Vercel hosting
Timeline Deposit's in, design kickoff now, standard build cycle once content lands

Not in Scope

  • Dedicated Birthday Parties, Memberships, Plan Your Visit, or About pages
  • Blog / news section
  • Standalone landing page
  • Embedded booking — Rhomb is link-out only, everywhere

Client & Audience

The Client

Who Pal's Park — a family-owned indoor playground opening in Wisconsin Rapids, WI — pre-launch, not yet operational
Contact Allison Brown Hewett, co-owner — she's the one making calls in practice
Tone w/ Us Casual and laid-back, slower response cadence — plan on some back-and-forth

The Audience

Who's Visiting Parents of young kids, mostly on mobile, checking this out before they trust an unstaffed space with their kid
What They Need Quick reassurance + practical info, then a fast path to booking on Rhomb
Key Insight There's no person at the door to vouch for the place — the site is the only handshake a parent gets before showing up
💬
The vibe, in one sentence

Warm, playful, and trustworthy — a family-run local spot that feels like self-service done right, not a corporate arcade.

Let's Talk

☕
Bring instincts, not just answers

These are conversation starters for our kickoff, not a checklist. You've got creative freedom here — I just want to make sure you've got the full picture first.

Given the brand guide isn't in hand yet, what would you want to see before locking any real color or type direction?

This is effectively a trial round — Pookie's handling brand/voice separately and her material may land mid-design. Flag anything you'd want confirmed before investing real time in specifics.

How do we signal "unstaffed but trustworthy" without the usual staffed-venue trust cues?

A lot of that trust-building is riding on FAQ copy right now — curious if there's a visual language that reinforces it.

No space photos exist yet since the venue isn't open — how do we want to handle Home page imagery in the interim?

Flagging it since it's unresolved on our end, but Pookie or the client may already have a plan here (stock, renders, something else) — worth asking before assuming it's a gap we need to solve ourselves.
