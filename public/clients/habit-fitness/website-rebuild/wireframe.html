Two independent filters (muscle group AND/OR equipment type) plus free-text search across name + nicknames is what keeps the library findable as it grows from 30 to 300 machines — a single category tree would force the lat pulldown to live under either "Back" or "Cable" when it's both. Muscle-group chips toggle (multi-select); equipment is a second filter row; the grid re-filters live with no reload. On mobile the chips wrap and the search box stays pinned at the top. Every card: thumbnail + machine name + muscle badge + equipment badge. All entries managed in the one "Machine/Video" CMS dataset.
Build Rule — Locked Taxonomy
Muscle group and equipment type are locked dropdowns — staff pick from fixed lists, never free-type. Aliases (nicknames) and secondary muscles feed search only, they're not filters. This keeps the filters from fragmenting ("Back" vs "back" vs "Lats") and keeps staff entry idiotproof.

Mobile-first by definition — every scan is a phone at a machine. Facade player loads the video only on tap for speed. Title + muscle/equipment badges come straight from the same "Machine/Video" record that powers the library card. Description is optional.
QR Durability Rule
The QR encodes the stable /m/[slug] URL — decoupled from the video. Andy can swap the video anytime without reprinting a single sticker. Slugs are flat and human (e.g. lat-pulldown), never category-prefixed, so recategorizing never breaks a printed QR. Duplicate machines (three identical leg presses) = one record / one slug, all stickers point to it. Multi-exercise stations = one QR / one page for v1, key movements timestamped in the single video.
