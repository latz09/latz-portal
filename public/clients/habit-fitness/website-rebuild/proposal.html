You could throw up a Square site yourself — I know it, you know it. But you've got a gym to run and you want premium. Plus the Tutorial Video System — the part no Square or template site can match.

Scan a code on a machine and a clean, Habit-branded tutorial page opens — never raw YouTube. Behind it, a public, searchable video library: filter by muscle group, filter by equipment, search by nickname ("pulldown," "lat machine"). One simple dataset powers both the machine pages and the library, so it scales from 30 machines to 300 without ever getting messy. Swap a video anytime and the codes never change — no reprinting.

Where the line is: Each machine gets its own QR code, generated straight from your site — I hand you the full set at launch, labeled by machine and ready for a print shop. Need one for a new machine later? It's a click in your dashboard. Getting them printed as stickers and onto the machines is on your side — any local print shop does it cheap — and that part isn't in the price. But the codes themselves come done and dialed; nothing for you to figure out.

It's a real reason to walk in, a real reason to stay, and a feature your competition flat-out doesn't have. Pricing for it is on the next page — bundled or core-only, your call.

03 Why This Site Matters

Nobody picks a gym off a feature list. They pick the one that looks like their kind of place before they ever walk in. Your best-fit members search you, glance at the site, and decide in about ten seconds whether Habit is for them.

A cleaner, more premium site makes that call easy. When the pricing is obvious and the culture that sets you apart hits right away, the right people know in seconds — and text you. You're at 300 members, chasing 500. A sharper front door is how you close that gap.

Here's the math, and it's deliberately conservative: at about $70 a month, one member is worth roughly $840 a year — and they renew. That puts the core build at about six new members' first year of dues, the full build at about eight. A site built to convert clears that easily — and every member it brings in past that point is recurring revenue, month after month, for as long as the site runs.
