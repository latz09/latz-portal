5 Tax Moves Every Small Business Should Make Before Year-End
Published April 15, 2026 · 5 min read
📝
Cover image (optional)
from Sanity
Tax season doesn't have to be a scramble. The businesses that come out ahead aren't the ones who panic in March — they're the ones who make smart moves throughout the year. Here are five things you can do right now to set yourself up for a stronger tax position.
1. Review Your Estimated Tax Payments
If your income has changed significantly this year — up or down — your quarterly estimated payments might be off. Underpaying leads to penalties. Overpaying means you're giving the IRS an interest-free loan. Neither is ideal. Pull your year-to-date P&L and compare it to what you've been paying in. Adjust now, not in January.
2. Max Out Your Retirement Contributions
SEP-IRAs, Solo 401(k)s, and SIMPLE IRAs all have contribution deadlines tied to your tax filing. But the planning starts now. If you haven't maxed out your contributions, you're leaving tax-deferred growth — and a deduction — on the table.
Inline image — chart, infographic, or supporting visual
uploaded via Sanity rich text editor
3. Accelerate Deductions (or Defer Income)
This is the classic year-end move: if you expect to be in a higher bracket this year, pull expenses forward. Prepay January rent, stock up on supplies, or make that equipment purchase you've been putting off. On the flip side, if next year looks leaner, consider deferring some December invoicing into January.
4. Clean Up Your Books
Uncategorized transactions, missing receipts, personal expenses mixed in with business — these all create problems at tax time. A clean set of books means a faster, cheaper, and more accurate tax return. If your books are behind, now is the time to catch up. Not February.
5. Schedule a Year-End Review with Your CPA
The best tax strategy isn't a last-minute deduction — it's a year-round relationship with someone who knows your business. A 30-minute year-end review can surface opportunities you'd never catch on your own: entity structure changes, depreciation elections, carryforward losses. Don't wait until you're filing.
