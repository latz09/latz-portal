Fast mover. Contact form to call in under 24 hours. Chris is clearly motivated — he's got business cards being printed and doesn't want them pointing to nothing. This is a "buy now" mindset, not a "let me think about it" client. Don't let the proposal sit. Get it out fast and close.

Communication Style

Chris is a tradesman, not a marketer. He explicitly asked for straight talk: "if it's a stupid way to do it, just tell me." He doesn't know web design at all — "I've never seen a page like that, so it's kind of hard for me to actually say what to do." He needs to be led, not consulted. Designer leads creative. The ICT Dynamic

The ICT partnership is the core of Chris's pitch. Everything on his business card, his elevator pitch, his website — it all leads back to "I'm partnered with ICT." ICT is the credibility. Chris is the face and the relationship. The site needs to make this feel seamless — not like a referral, but like a unified team. The trust section is arguably the most important section on the page.

Pricing Strategy

Chris said his budget was $500–$1,000. On the call, Jordan said "at most $1,000." After full discovery and wireframing, scope landed at $1,200. This is slightly above what was verbally discussed — proposal needs to justify the value clearly. The CMS add-on at $600 brings total to $1,800 which is well within his "up to $10,000" ceiling but above his comfortable range. Lead with the $1,200 base, position CMS as optional.

Growth Potential

This client has real expansion potential. He mentioned solar, more pages, updating content, a bigger web presence. If CK Power takes off (generators + trimlight + solar in central WI is a solid market), this $1,200 starter site could turn into a $3,000–$5,000 multi-page build within a year. Treat this as a long-term relationship, not a one-off gig.

Designer Involvement

Jordan mentioned on the call: "I'll talk with my designer as well... she's amazing. She has a long background in this type of stuff." Designer allocation is ~$200 internal. Designer is leading creative direction since Chris has no inspiration sites and no design opinions. Need to make sure designer has the logo and ICT context before starting visual exploration.
