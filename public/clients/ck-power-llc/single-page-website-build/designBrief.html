Brand & Visual Direction

🚨
Hard rule — zero generator imagery or references, anywhere Chris's day job creates a legal conflict that prevents CK Power from marketing generator work. No generator photos, no Generac/Kohler logos, no "we install generators" language, no transfer-switch imagery. Anything power-generation lives under ICT Electric in the partnership section — that's the only path. Keep this in mind for stock photo selection especially.
Logo — Received
CK POWER
Energy and Power

Two-tone industrial mark

Split lightbulb with embedded lightning bolt — left half bright blue, right half dark navy, radiating energy lines. Bold italic typeface, forward-leaning, mixed case. Tagline "ENERGY AND POWER" in bright blue italic. Files in hand: PNG on dark background, PDF on white. No vector yet — request SVG from Chris if you want to scale clean for hero treatments. The logo gives you a strong, opinionated starting point — extend it confidently.

Electric Blue
#0094DE
CTAs, links, accents, energy moments. The "voltage" color.
Deep Navy
#1F3B6A
Headings, dark sections, nav, body grounding.

Heads up — these hex values came from a quick color-picker pass over the logo PNG, not from a brand spec sheet. Treat them as close-but-not-gospel. If you want exact values, pull them yourself from the source file or nudge them to whatever reads cleanest on screen. Two-color palette as the spine, neutrals (white, off-white, gray) for everything else. Open to a third accent if you want to introduce one — warm orange or a metallic tone could play nicely against the blues. Your call.

What We Know About the Vibe
FeelIndustrial, confident, masculine. Forward-leaning energy — the italic logo says it all.
Not ThisCorporate-slick SaaS aesthetic. Generic contractor template. Cute or playful. Anything that softens the blue-collar edge.
Hero MoveTrimlight is a visual product — it sells on imagery. The hero needs to make someone go "oh, that's cool" before they read a word.
Trust AnchorICT partnership section is a credibility play — license numbers, "30+ years." Treat it like a badge moment, not an afterthought.
Reference — Your Creative Sandbox

Chris had no inspiration sites — said nothing caught his eye. Designer leads creative direction here. That means real latitude on type, hero treatment, dark/light section rhythm, and how the Trimlight imagery is staged. Lean into the blue-collar industrial side rather than softening it.

  • What does dark navy + electric blue look like at scale — full-bleed dark sections vs. light?
  • What typeface pairs with that italic logo without competing with it?
  • How do we stage Trimlight imagery so it carries the visual weight of a product the homeowner can imagine on their house?
  • What does the ICT partnership "trust badge" moment look like — license numbers as the hero of that section?

Let's Talk

☕
Bring instincts, not just answers The logo gives you a strong starting point and the audience is well-defined — so the open questions are about how far to push the industrial vibe and where the visual weight sits. These are conversation starters. You know design better than I do.
Open Design Questions
How dark do we go?
The logo lives natively on dark navy. Curious where you want to land — full-bleed dark hero with electric-blue accents, or a lighter site with strategic dark sections (services, partnership)? Both could work. The blue-collar industrial vibe pulls toward dark; the "approachable for a homeowner" pulls toward light. Want your read.
Typography to pair with the logo
The wordmark is a bold italic — opinionated. Body needs to feel industrial but not fight it. Curious whether you want to lean into a condensed display face for headings or stay more neutral and let the logo carry the personality. Click-to-call usability matters — body needs to be readable on a phone in daylight.
Hero treatment — what carries the weight?
Trimlight at night is the obvious move, but every Trimlight installer's site does the same thing. Worth thinking about: is there a way to stage it that feels more "central WI residential" than "anonymous suburban house"? Stock will be the constraint here — see what's findable, then decide if a stylized treatment (gradient overlay, motion, type-led) saves us from a flat photo.
The ICT partnership section — credibility moment
This section is doing real work — it's where anyone needing electrical/power gets routed and where new-business credibility gets established. License numbers and "30+ years" are the trust currency. Worth treating it like a badge moment with visual weight, not a referral footnote. Curious how you want to stage it.
Accent color — yes or no?
Two blues is solid but tight. Open to a third accent if you want one — could be a warm contrast (orange, amber) or a metallic note. Or hold the line at two and let neutrals breathe. Your instinct.
Mobile-first hero — what survives the shrink?
Most traffic is going to be someone holding a business card and tapping the URL on their phone. The hero needs to read fast and convert fast on a 5-inch screen. Worth thinking about that constraint up front rather than retrofitting from a desktop comp.
