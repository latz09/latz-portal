Project Snapshot

The job

Give Irish Hospital Apartments a landing page that finally matches the building. It's a converted 1930s hospital in Forest City, Iowa — fully occupied since November 2022 — and right now the site undersells that story. One page needs to do three things: hook a curious visitor with the real history, make applying feel like an easy next step, and get current residents to rent pay or maintenance requests fast, with zero friction.

The Build
ScopeSingle landing page — Hero, Building Story, Amenities, Resident Services

Client & Audience

The Client
Who She IsKristen Fish-Peterson personally led the two-year adaptive reuse of this building — nine sources in the capital stack. She's not a distant property manager; she's the person who made this place happen.
Our ContactKristen, Owner — decisive, moves fast once committed, done waiting on a slow vendor.
ToneWarm, historic, a little proud. Not corporate, not generic-apartment-complex.
PositioningWorkforce housing — real and unpretentious, not luxury-market.
The Audience
ProspectsDeciding whether to apply. Mobile-heavy, casual browsing — this is the story-and-persuasion audience.
Current ResidentsRepeat visitors who just want to pay rent or file a maintenance request. Speed over persuasion.
DevicesMostly mobile for prospects; mixed for residents returning to pay rent.
Key InsightThe building is always fully occupied — there's rarely urgency to "sell" vacancy. Tone can lean toward pride and character rather than a hard sales push.
💡
Vibe in one sentence A small-town Iowa landmark, brought back to life — proud of its history, confident without being closed-off, nothing sterile or corporate about it.

Brand & Visual Direction

🚪
Real history, wide-open direction — this is the fun one Kristen isn't attached to the current mark. She's fully on board with refining it or reworking it entirely, as long as it stays grounded in the real building — not generic apartment-marketing aesthetics. Full latitude on logo, palette, and supporting graphics.
The Real Story — This Is the Actual Brand Asset
ThenBuilt in the 1930s as a hospital in Forest City, IA. Later became a law enforcement center. Then sat vacant.
The TurnKristen personally led a two-year adaptive reuse — nine sources in the capital stack — to bring it back as apartments.
NowFully occupied since November 2022. A landmark building doing real work in a town of ~4,000, home to Winnebago Industries.
Why It MattersThis history is worth more than any logo or color choice — it's the emotional hook the whole page should hang on, more than an amenity list ever could.

Let's Talk

Bring your instincts — not just answers This brand direction is wide open, and that's the exciting part. These are conversation starters, not a checklist — if you've got a direction you want to run with, let's hear it.
Open Design Questions
How far do we push the "converted hospital" story visually?
Is there a tasteful way to nod to that history — a detail, a motif, a subtle callback — without it feeling gimmicky or like a theme park?
Is the doorknob-plate logo worth keeping in spirit, or does this need a fresh mark?
Kristen's genuinely open either way. If the badge concept still serves the story once refined, great — if a new mark tells it better, that's on the table too.
How do we handle "always full" in tone?
Proud and confident without sounding closed-off to a genuinely interested prospect. This isn't a hard sell — but it shouldn't feel unwelcoming either.
Does the current crimson/brown/stone palette earn its place?
Or is there something that better captures small-town, historic-building Iowa — Forest City, not a luxury urban rehab.
