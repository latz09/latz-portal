01 A Note From Jordan
Kristen,

The current site was built in 2022 by Accent Graphix, and it's actively working against you. It's shown a unit as "available" when all 8 have been occupied, rent amounts on the page are stale, and when a tenant's payment failed last year, the vendor's response was to blame you for not installing updates — on a site where you were never given admin access to install anything. Now they want $500 to fix accessibility issues they created in the first place. That's not a partnership, that's a hostage situation with your own website.

Irish Hospital Apartments doesn't have a leasing problem — the building is always full. What it has is a trust and presentation problem. A 1930s hospital converted into 8 units is a genuinely great story, and right now nothing online tells it. Meanwhile the one job the current site is supposed to do reliably — point residents and prospects to rent, apply, and get maintenance help — it does badly.

I've been playing with a direction that leans into the building's real history — a 1930s hospital converted into 8 units — rather than a generic apartment-listing look. Nothing's been designed yet, but it's the angle I'd recommend, and I'm happy to walk you through it once we've locked in scope here.

This proposal outlines a single, branded landing page that tells the building's story properly and gives residents a clean, reliable way to apply, pay rent, and submit maintenance requests. No live availability grid to go stale again — just what's true, presented well, and easy for you to keep working.

03 Why This Site Matters

Irish Hospital Apartments doesn't need a site that fills vacancies — it's always full. What it needs is a site that doesn't actively work against you and your residents. Right now, the current one does both: showing units as available when they're not, displaying stale rent figures, and — in one real incident — leaving a tenant's payment to fail while the vendor pinned the blame on you for updates you were never given the access to make.

That's the real cost of the current site — not lost leads, but lost trust, on top of a vendor now trying to charge you $500 to fix problems of their own making.

A site that simply tells the truth, presents the building's story with the credibility it deserves, and gives residents a clean path to apply, pay, and get help — that's the bar here. It's not a high bar. The current site doesn't clear it.
