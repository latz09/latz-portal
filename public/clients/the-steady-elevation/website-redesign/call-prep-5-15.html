Resources / Shop · The Payment Fork

The key question: external links or on-site checkout.
External Links
simpler
What It Is

Resources page lists her book (and anything else) with a "Buy on Amazon" button that opens the existing storefront in a new tab. Customers pay Amazon, not her site.

Pros
  • Faster to build, no payment infrastructure
  • Uses existing Amazon reviews / KDP system
  • No tax / fulfillment to worry about on her end
  • Amazon handles refunds and customer service
Tradeoff

Sends traffic away. She doesn't capture the customer email or the full margin. Looks more like a portfolio than a store.

On-Site (Stripe)
key fork
What It Is

Customers checkout directly on her site. Stripe handles the payment. She gets the customer email, full margin, and the relationship.

Pros
  • Captures customer email — builds her list
  • Keeps the full margin (minus Stripe fees ~3%)
  • Looks and feels like a real storefront
  • Ready for digital products (webinars, PDFs) later
Tradeoff

More setup. She handles fulfillment for physical items (or use print-on-demand). For digital products, fulfillment is automatic — that's where this really pays off.

Shop / Payments Questions
"What's the real difference — external vs. on-site?"
⤴
  • External: simple, Amazon handles everything
  • On-site: keeps customer relationship + margin
  • Digital products tip toward on-site
"What does Stripe cost?"
⤴
  • ~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
  • No monthly fee, no setup fee
  • Industry standard, same as Square / PayPal
"How would I ship the book if I sell on the site?"
⤴
  • Ship herself OR use print-on-demand
  • Amazon link = simpler if she doesn't want fulfillment
  • Hybrid: physical via Amazon, digital on-site
"What about selling digital — like a webinar?"
⤴
  • Digital is where on-site Stripe really pays off
  • Auto-delivery via download link or email
  • No shipping, no inventory, no fulfillment
"Do I have to deal with sales tax?"
⤴

  • Worth a CPA conversation, not a web-dev one
