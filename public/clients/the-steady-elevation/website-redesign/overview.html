Project Summary

Full website redesign — not a patch job.

Diagnosed the existing site Feb 25 and proposed a full redesign over patchwork edits. Stephanie agreed. 23 pages, 10 shared templates, two fully-themed sides (Individual + Business), Sanity CMS, Vercel hosting. Designer: Alyssa Shurbert-Hetzel.

Why a Redesign (Not Edits)

  • 🔴Existing site had fundamental structure problems not worth patching
  • 🔴Two audiences (Individual + Business) not clearly differentiated
  • 🔴No CMS — Stephanie couldn't manage events or testimonials herself
  • 🔴Site didn't reflect the brand depth or service breadth

What Success Looks Like

  • ✅Two clearly distinct sides — Individual (sage) and Business (navy/teal)
  • ✅Stephanie manages events + testimonials herself via Sanity
  • ✅Calendly drives bookings — zero friction to schedule
  • ✅Cohesive brand — Climora Thin heading font, Raleway body
  • ✅10 reusable templates = efficient build + consistent experience
🔀
The Fork at / is the entire UX strategy No nav on the root page. Full-screen split: visitor self-selects Individual or Business. Once inside a side, the entire color system + content shifts. Logo links to current side hub — not back to the fork. Footer has a subtle "Switch to Business / Individual" escape hatch.
⚠️
NOT in scope: Pricing pages No pricing displayed anywhere on the site — including The Elevation Series. This is a firm decision. Don't second-guess it.

Notes & Observations

Feb 25, 2026 — Decision to Redesign

Stephanie paid the deposit the same day she got the diagnosis. Fast decision, clear trust in Jordan's judgment. She wasn't attached to the old site — she agreed immediately when presented with "full redesign, not patchwork edits." That's a good client. Respect the trust by executing cleanly.

Apr 9, 2026 — Font Change Note

Champagne & Limousines was cut because it was too hard to read at small sizes. Raleway confirmed as the replacement for body + subheadings. This was a practical call, not a brand direction change — document it so no one goes back and questions it.

On the Fork Concept

The fork at / is the whole UX bet. If a visitor self-selects into a side, every subsequent page they see is filtered to their context. This only works if the fork itself is clear, visually balanced, and makes both sides feel equally premium. Don't let one side feel like the "real" site and the other feel like a sub-section. They must feel like two distinct presences that share a root.

On Alyssa's Role

Alyssa is the designer — Jordan and Alyssa coordinate directly. Stephanie reviews finished work but isn't in the day-to-day design loop. Keep the design conversation clean: don't put Stephanie in a position to weigh in on in-progress design decisions she doesn't have the context to evaluate. Bring her polished outputs, not process.

Content Support — Define This Before Launch

Three months of content support is included in the contract, but exactly what that means hasn't been documented in these notes. Before the final invoice goes out, make sure Stephanie knows what's included — number of rounds, types of changes, what's out of scope. Ambiguity here will cause friction post-launch.
