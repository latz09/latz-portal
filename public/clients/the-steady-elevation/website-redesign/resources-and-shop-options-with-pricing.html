02 How The Shop Works (Either Option)

A quick framing before the options, since both share the same shape:

For your customer: They browse the Resources page on either side of the site (Individual or Business), click "Buy" on a product, pay, and get their file or recording automatically. Same experience either way.

For you: You add products in the CMS — title, image, short description, which side(s) it shows on, price. The shop runs the same way regardless of which option you pick.

What you can sell: PDFs, downloadable files, and webinar recordings — all handled the same way. For webinars, you can either upload the video file directly (customer downloads it) or paste in a private Vimeo/YouTube link (customer gets a viewing link). Your call per product. The book listing can also live on the Resources page, linking out to wherever you fulfill it.

03 Two Storefront Options
Option 1

Lemon Squeezy Storefront

5% + 50¢ per sale  ·  No monthly fee

How it works for you

  • You sign up for Lemon Squeezy (free), invite me as a team member
  • I set up your first 3–5 products — files, pricing, checkout
  • 30-min screenshare: I show you how to add the next one yourself
  • You add resources to your site via the CMS — paste in the Lemon Squeezy checkout URL and you're done

How it works for your customer

  • Clicks "Buy" — Lemon Squeezy checkout opens in a new tab
  • Pays, gets the download link emailed automatically
  • Returned to your Resources page after payment

Why I lean toward this

  • Zero technical liability — they handle delivery, refunds, receipts
  • Sales tax handled completely (they're a Merchant of Record — collect & remit in every state and country for you)
  • Faster to launch, simpler to maintain
  • Reversible — we can upgrade to Option 2 later if volume grows

On a $15 PDF, you keep $13.75 per sale.

Option 2

Custom Stripe Storefront

2.9% + 30¢ per sale  ·  No monthly fee, no platform cut

How it works for you

  • You sign up for Stripe (free), invite me as a team member
  • I set up your first 3–5 products and build the integration
  • 60-min screenshare: how to add new products (~3 min in Stripe, paste IDs into CMS)
  • You upload the file directly in the CMS (or paste a viewing link for webinars)

How it works for your customer

  • Clicks "Buy" — Stripe-hosted checkout, branded with your colors
  • Pays, your site auto-sends a secure download link (expires in 7 days)
  • Returned to your Resources page after payment

Why you'd pick this

  • Single source of truth — everything customer-facing lives in your CMS
  • Lower per-sale fee long-term (~$0.52 more per $15 sale than Option 1)
  • Foundation for growth — easier to extend into paid event registration, memberships, gated library

On a $15 PDF, you keep $14.27 per sale.

⚠ Real Difference: Sales Tax

Unlike Lemon Squeezy, Stripe doesn't handle sales tax — that responsibility lives with you. Two things worth knowing:

1. Minnesota tax from day one. MN considers digital products (e-books, audio, video) taxable, and because you operate the business from MN, you have automatic physical nexus. State + local rates run ~6.875%–8.875% on MN customers.

2. Other states aren't an immediate concern. Most require registration only after $100K in annual sales OR 200 transactions in that state — thresholds you'd be a long way from at $15 PDFs.

Options if it grows: Stripe Tax (built-in, 0.5%/transaction — I can wire this in ready to enable) or a service like TaxJar ($19–50/mo, overkill at low volume). Real homework you'd own with Option 2 — flagging it because it's a genuine difference.

04 Event Interest Capture (Add-On)
Works with either option

Email Signup On Event Cards

You mentioned wanting to capture interest in upcoming events before you announce dates. Visitors see an event card, drop in their email, and you build the list inside the CMS. Toggle it on per event — only the ones you choose collect emails.

  • Toggle on each event for "Collect Interest" — you decide which events have the form
  • Email signup form on event cards when the toggle is on
  • Email list stored on the event in the CMS
  • CSV export button right inside the CMS — paste into a BCC field or share however you want

Future option (not included): If you ever want to pipe these emails directly into Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or similar so they flow into a real list automatically, that's a separate add-on I can quote later. For launch, simple CMS storage + export covers what you described wanting.
