⟶ How Purchasing Works
The Buying Experience
What your customers see when they order a book — from "Add to Cart" to delivery.
🛒 The shopping experience lives on your site — browsing, adding to cart, reviewing the cart all happen here. For the actual payment step, the customer will likely hand off to a hosted payment page (Stripe or similar) and come back to your site once they're done. The exact handoff and what happens after a successful order — confirmation emails, where customer data lives, how Jeannine gets notified — is still being worked out. The shape below is the rough plan, not a locked-in spec.
Step 1 — Browse & Add to Cart
Where it happens: On your site — jeanninekellogg.com
Customer is on a book page or the Shop page. They click "Add to Cart" — the book gets added to their cart without leaving the page. Everything looks and feels like your site because it is your site.

Step 3 — Checkout & Payment
Where it happens: Likely a hosted payment page — Stripe or similar (TBD)
When the customer clicks "Checkout," they'll hand off to a secure hosted payment page to enter their shipping address and credit card. The page is branded as much as the platform allows, but it lives on the payment provider's domain — not yours. Once the payment goes through, they auto-redirect back to your site to a confirmation page.
Using a hosted page means you don't have to handle card data directly — the payment provider takes on all the PCI compliance. Tradeoff: the customer is briefly off your domain.

Step 4 — Order Confirmed & Fulfilled
What happens after a successful payment — rough plan:
↩️
Customer auto-redirects back to your site to a confirmation / thank-you page.
✉️
Customer gets a receipt — probably sent by the payment provider, possibly also from the site. Exact source TBD.
🔔
Jeannine gets a notification that someone bought. Likely an automated email triggered by the site or the payment provider. Method TBD.
🗄️
Order data (name, shipping address, items, etc.) lives somewhere Jeannine can access — could be the site's CMS, could be the payment provider's dashboard, could be both. Still figuring out the right home for it.
📦
Jeannine enters the order into Stacks, which routes it to Itasca / Bookmobile for drop-ship fulfillment.
🚚
Itasca / Bookmobile picks, packs, and ships the book directly to the customer. You never touch inventory.
This whole post-purchase flow is the rough sketch, not a locked design. Real questions to resolve: Where does the order data live — site CMS, payment provider, or both? Who sends the customer the receipt? How does Jeannine find out a sale happened, and how fast? We'll figure these out together before build.
Post-Purchase Flow — TBD
Where order info lives, who sends what email to whom, and how Jeannine gets notified after a sale are all open questions. Some of this depends on which payment provider we land on — Stripe's dashboard might end up being Jeannine's de facto order database, or we might pull it all into the site's CMS. Pending follow-up.
The Rough Plan
Customers browse and shop on your custom site. When it's time to pay, they hand off to a hosted payment page (Stripe or similar) and come back to your site once the order goes through. You get notified of the sale, the order details land somewhere you can access them, and you enter the order into Stacks for Itasca / Bookmobile to ship. The exact wiring between site, payment provider, and notifications is still being figured out — this is the rough shape, not the final spec.
