Research Findings

What I Found Out About Itasca's System

I dug into Bookmobile's documentation and public information about how their fulfillment integration actually works. Here's the key finding:

Bookmobile doesn't give developers an API to push orders into their system. It works the other way around — their system automatically pulls pending orders from your ecommerce platform on an hourly basis. And they only support four platforms: Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and SquareSpace.

Your current site is built on Next.js, which isn't on that list. That means we can't just wire up a direct connection between your website and Itasca. We need one of their supported platforms in the mix.

If none of those platforms work, the only alternatives are manually uploading orders to their Stacks portal one-by-one or via spreadsheet — which defeats the purpose of having an automated online store.

This is actually good news.

It means the integration between your store and Bookmobile is a solved problem. Their IT team has done this setup many times with other publishers. We're not building something experimental — we're plugging into a system that already works.

Recommendation

Why Shopify — and How It Fits

I know adding Shopify might sound like we're introducing a whole separate website platform. We're not. Here's what Shopify actually does in this setup:

Shopify is the backend only. It's the database that holds your book catalog (titles, prices, descriptions, inventory status) and the payment processor that handles credit card transactions. Think of it as the engine under the hood — customers never interact with Shopify directly except for the final checkout screen where they enter payment info.

Your website — the design, the book pages, the branding, the entire experience — is still fully custom-built by me. Nothing about the look and feel changes. Shopify just provides two things we need: a product database that Bookmobile can connect to, and secure payment processing.

The reason I'm recommending Shopify specifically over WooCommerce or BigCommerce: it's the most widely used of Bookmobile's supported platforms, which means the most proven integration path. It's also the simplest for you to manage on the backend — adding a book or changing a price is straightforward.

Why not build a custom connection?

We could try to build a direct integration between your Next.js site and Itasca's system, but it wouldn't be on their supported platform list. That means their IT team can't set it up through their standard process, there's no guarantee it would work reliably, and if something breaks, we'd be on our own troubleshooting it. Using Shopify means we get their standard, tested connection — and their IT support if anything goes sideways.
