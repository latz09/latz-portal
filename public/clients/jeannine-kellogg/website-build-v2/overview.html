Notes & Observations

Apr 16, 2026 — Re-Engagement

She came back. That's significant. After an 18-month gap + publishing disaster, she emailed directly with a specific ask (not a vague "checking in"). She's treating me as her web person by default — don't take that for granted, but also don't be coy. Reply today. Confirm availability. Propose a call.

$275 — Strategic Writeoff

Decision: apply the outstanding $275 from Invoice 0118 as a credit/writeoff and mention it in the v2 sell as a goodwill gesture. Framing: "I'm closing out the old invoice as a credit toward v2 — let's focus on getting the relaunch right." Costs nothing in cash terms (it was never collected) but buys meaningful relationship capital right when we need her to say yes to a bigger v2 number. Don't lead with it — drop it at the close.

Ecommerce Decision — Biggest Lever in v2

Custom Next.js preserves the "no monthly fees, own it forever" pitch that landed v1. Shopify breaks that promise. But Shopify is faster/cheaper upfront and has pre-built Itasca integration. The honest recommendation depends on (1) how clean Itasca's API docs are — get them before quoting, (2) her expected order volume — low volume = Shopify makes sense, high volume = custom pays for itself, (3) Fuzzy Duck's opinion — marketing agencies default to Shopify. Bring the comparison table to the discovery call and let her pick with eyes open.

Fuzzy Duck Dynamic

A marketing agency is now in the picture. This can go two ways: (1) they respect the tech/web boundary and we coordinate cleanly, or (2) they try to insert themselves into site design decisions. Early call with Jeannine should clarify who does what. Ideal: Fuzzy Duck = brand/marketing/cover design, Latz = site build/ecommerce. Get a direct contact at FD so coordination doesn't always route through Jeannine.

Shopify Tradeoff — Worth Naming Directly

If she leans Shopify, don't dance around it — be upfront that it introduces monthly fees (contrary to the v1 "no monthly fees" pitch) and frame it as a deliberate tradeoff for speed/simplicity/lower upfront cost. Transparency here protects the brand and respects her intelligence. Don't hide the math.

She's Resilient — Frame This as a Relaunch

Her email is remarkable — matter-of-fact about losing a 2,400-book deal and a catastrophic print run, then "I have risen from the stormy seas." This is someone who's ready to push through. The v2 proposal should match that energy: this isn't a patch job, it's a relaunch. Position the website as part of the comeback.

Original v1 Vision — Still Relevant

The v1 proposal emphasized "eye-popping design," "wow factor on landing page," artwork throughout, building credibility for parents searching after book fairs. That vision is still the goal — v2 just adds ecommerce + a third book. Don't reinvent the strategy. Build on it.

Client Communication Style

Jeannine writes long, clear, detailed emails. She tells you exactly what's going on — publishing issues, marketing agency, artist timeline, distributor's new API, the specific quote from Itasca. That's gold. Match her style: detailed, transparent, no sales fluff. She respects competence.
