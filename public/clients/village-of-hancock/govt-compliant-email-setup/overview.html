Platform Decision: M365 vs Google Workspace

🔑
The deciding factor: cross-mailbox search for open records requests Jillian's exact words: "if someone open records requests us we need to be able to have the clerk search all of our email boxes for specific topics." Whatever platform we pick, this feature must be included — not an expensive add-on.
Feature Microsoft 365 Business Basic Google Workspace (Vault required)
Cross-Mailbox Search ✅ Content Search included — admin can search all 15 mailboxes by keyword ⚠️ Requires Vault — only included in Business Plus ($22/user)
Price Per User (current) $6/user/month ($7 after July 1, 2026) $22/user/month (Business Plus — the only tier with Vault)
Monthly Cost (15 users) $90/mo (→ $105 after July '26) $330/mo
Annual Cost (15 users) ~$1,080/yr (→ $1,260 after July '26) ~$3,960/yr
eDiscovery / Legal Hold Content Search included. Full eDiscovery (Standard) requires E3 — likely overkill for this village. Vault included in Business Plus. Search, retention rules, legal holds, export.
Government Plans GCC exists but requires entity verification + paperwork. Standard commercial plan is likely sufficient for a ~416-person village. No specific government tier for Workspace.
Admin Simplicity Admin center is straightforward. Clerk can manage users. Google Admin console is clean and intuitive. Slightly easier UX for non-tech users.
Jordan's Familiarity Less familiar — would need to learn M365 admin center, Purview portal More familiar with Google ecosystem
Verdict 🏆 WINNER on price — $240/mo cheaper. Content Search covers the core need. Better UX, but 3.6× the cost for the plan that includes cross-mailbox search.
Recommendation: Microsoft 365 Business Basic @ $6–7/user/month

Google Workspace only includes the cross-mailbox search (Vault) at the Business Plus tier — $22/user. That's $330/month for 15 users vs $90–105 on M365 Business Basic, which includes Content Search out of the box. The cost difference is $2,700–$3,000/year. For a village of 416 people, that budget difference matters.

However: Jordan is more familiar with Google Workspace. If you want to use Google anyway, the village would need to be on Business Plus ($22/user) — make sure the client understands the cost difference. The $400 setup fee is the same either way.

🏛️
GCC (Government Community Cloud) — Likely Not Required Microsoft has GCC plans for government entities, but these require entity verification and add complexity. For a small Wisconsin village with ~416 residents, standard M365 Business Basic with Content Search should meet their compliance needs for open records requests. GCC is designed for agencies handling controlled unclassified information (CUI) or needing FedRAMP compliance — overkill here. If there's any doubt, verify with the village's legal counsel before setup.
💡
Pricing Lock-In Opportunity M365 Business Basic goes from $6 → $7/user on July 1, 2026. If the village signs up on an annual commitment before then, they lock in $6/user for the first year. That's $1,080/year instead of $1,260. Worth mentioning to Jillian as a gentle nudge to get the board directory over sooner.
