03 Why This Matters
— News & Events —

The clerk finally gets a real way to reach residents.

Right now, if there's a road closure, a meeting change, or a community event the village wants to promote, there's no central place to put it. Residents have to call the office or rely on word of mouth. That's the gap.

A News & Events page solves it with one tool. The clerk writes the update once, it appears on the page, and a preview lands on the home page automatically. Anyone with the link knows what's going on. No phone calls, no missed notices, no "I didn't hear about that" at the next board meeting.

— Hancock Park —

The kayak kiosk needs a home. So does the rest of the park.

The board approved the kayak rental kiosk specifically to generate revenue. That only works if people can find it. A paragraph on the About page won't get indexed, won't get linked from Travel Wisconsin, and won't get shared on Facebook. A real Hancock Park page does all three.

The kiosk, the campground, the beach, Fish Lake, the boat ramp — these are the actual reasons people drive to Hancock. Pulling them out of a buried paragraph and giving them a proper page means the village can point anywhere — flyers, Facebook, regional tourism sites — and have something to link to. And every section is CMS-managed, so seasonal rates, hours, and details get updated by the clerk in seconds, every spring.
