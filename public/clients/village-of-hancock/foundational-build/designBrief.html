Project Snapshot

The job

Give the Village of Hancock a professional, accessible home online. Residents need to find meeting agendas, minutes, and ordinances without calling anyone. The board needs to feel proud to share the link. Right now there's nothing — the old site is broken and unreachable.

The Build
Scope5-page custom site — Foundation + Documents
CMSSanity — documents and board directory
StackNext.js, React, Tailwind, Vercel
Timeline3–5 weeks with prompt content delivery

News/NoticesNo bulletin board — future upsell

Client & Audience

The Client
Who They AreSmall Wisconsin village, ~380 people. Part-time elected board. Not tech-forward.
Our ContactJillian Steele, Trustee — enthusiastic, responsive, not technical. The project champion.
Who Updates SiteThe village clerk — CMS must be dead simple.
ToneWarm, civic, trustworthy. Small-town — not corporate, not government-cold.
The Audience
PrimaryVillage residents — skew 60+. Many not comfortable with technology.
What They NeedAgendas, minutes, ordinances, board contact info. Fast and easy to find.
DevicesMix of desktop and mobile. Older devices, slower connections.
Key InsightIf a 70-year-old who doesn't love computers can find it in 2 clicks, we won.
💡
Vibe in one sentence Small-town Wisconsin civic pride — warm, clean, and trustworthy. Think lakeside community, not city hall.

Let's Talk

☕
Bring your instincts — not just answers These are things worth talking through before we're deep in build mode. You know design better than I do — if you've got a direction you want to run with, let's hear it. These are conversation starters, not a checklist.
Open Design Questions
What's the color direction?
No existing brand, open slate. Lake country Wisconsin — what palette feels right for a small, warm, lakeside village? Would love to see 2–3 directions to kick off the conversation. Blue is obvious — what's more interesting?
Typography — warm and readable without being boring
Needs to feel trustworthy and human without being stiff. Large, clear body text is a given for this audience. What do you pair with headings that still has some character?
How do we handle the hero without a professional photo?
We'll get what we get from Jillian — likely casual community shots. How does the hero hold up? Color overlay, illustrated element, pattern? Worth thinking about before we're stuck with a bad cropped lake photo at full-width.
Does the village need a logo or wordmark?
Nothing polished exists — just the old seal. The nav needs something. Could be a clean wordmark, could be something more. Your instinct on how involved this gets would be helpful.
Does the seal belong in the design?
It's there, it's theirs, it has history. But it might be too dated to feature prominently. Could live quietly in the footer as a nod to the village's heritage — or left out entirely. Your read on it is what matters.
