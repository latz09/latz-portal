Notes & Observations

Budget Reality Check

Bill came in at $1,500. The scope he sent is a 5–6 page site with detailed service breakdowns, dual leadership bios, a capability statement system, and a multi-field contact form. That's $3,500–$5,000 work. The wireframe needs to show him what this site actually IS — and then the pricing needs to feel justified by the quality of what he sees. Don't lead with the number; lead with the vision.

Trust Factor — Handle with Care

Bill explicitly said he was burned by a previous developer who built something that was "never what I wanted." This means: (1) show, don't tell — the wireframe is critical, (2) get sign-off early and often, (3) share the dev link as you build so he sees progress, (4) don't overpromise. Earning trust is more important than closing fast.

The Credentials Are Legit

Tracy's background: Secret-level DoD missions, M1 Abrams tank transport, Army veteran. Bill: ARCOM medals, 1M+ accident-free miles, certified bookkeeper. These aren't puffed-up LinkedIn bios — this is real. The About page is potentially the most important page on the site. Procurement officers reading those bios will take PFL seriously. Design the About page to do maximum work.

Pitch Email Strategy

Lead with the wireframe (visual impact). Then: "Your content is already written — that saves significant time and brings the cost down. Here's what I'd recommend for Phase 1." Frame $3,500–$5,000 as the value play, not the anchor. Emphasize: no monthly fees ($20/mo hosting vs. $100–$300/mo), they own it forever, and their team's background deserves to be presented at the level of the companies they work alongside (Boeing, Halliburton, UPS).

Alaska Phone Number

Bill's number is 907 (Alaska area code) but he's based in Wausau, WI. Likely a number from prior military or work. Don't assume anything — just note it so it doesn't cause confusion if someone else sees it in the system.

The $1,500 Fallback — 3 Pages

Jordan floated a 3-page version at $1,500 to keep Bill in the conversation. If the full build doesn't land, the fallback is: Home + About + Contact for $1,500. It wouldn't include Services, Industries, or Capability Statement — which guts the project's value. Try hard to land the full build. The 3-page version is a last resort, not the pitch.

Logo Received — Open to Changes

Bill sent over the current PFL logo but noted he's open to other options. Don't pitch a redesign unprompted — review what he sent, assess whether it holds up in a professional federal-facing site context, and let the conversation develop naturally. If the logo looks dated or doesn't scale well for web, that becomes a soft upsell opportunity during the design phase. Refinement or full redesign could be bundled or done as a standalone add-on.
