03 Discovery Questions
0 of 10 answered ✓ Answers saved
What's PFL's story in your own words?
When you tell someone at a conference or in a meeting what Premier Federal Logistics does, what do you say? The way you naturally describe it is usually the clearest version — and it helps us figure out how the brand should introduce itself.
When someone sees your logo for the first time, what's the one thing you want them to feel or think?
This is the gut check that guides everything. "These people are serious." "I can trust this company." "This is a real operation." Whatever the instinct is — that's what the logo needs to trigger.
What's wrong with the current Fiverr logo?
Beyond just "it needs an upgrade" — what specifically doesn't work? Does it look too generic? Too small-time? Wrong colors? Wrong feel? Knowing what bugs you about it tells us exactly what to avoid.
Are there any logos or brands you admire — especially in your industry?
Federal contractors, defense companies, logistics firms, or really any brand that makes you think "that's the level we should be at." Links or screenshots are great — describe them here and send any images separately to my email.
Any logos or brand styles that turn you off?
Knowing what to avoid is just as valuable as knowing what you're drawn to. Logos you've seen that feel wrong, generic, cheap, or just not you.
How do you feel about the color direction — navy, red/white/blue, gold accents?
The overview mentioned patriotic undertones with navy as the base and gold/brass accents. Does that still feel right? Is there a color that's non-negotiable, or one you'd rather avoid? This is the time to steer.
Icon mark, monogram, text-only — any gut reaction?
We'll present multiple directions regardless, but if you already lean toward something — a "PFL" monogram, a graphic element, or just the company name styled right — that helps us prioritize. No wrong answer.
Do you have a tagline, or want one?
If you've got a tagline or a phrase you use (even informally), we can build a tagline lock-up as part of the logo system. If you don't have one and want to explore it, that's on the table too.
Who are your top 2–3 competitors? Anything about their branding you admire or want to avoid?
The designer will do a competitor review to make sure PFL's brand stands apart. Knowing who you're up against — and what they look like — helps us differentiate from the start.
Anything else we should know?
Gut feelings, random ideas, things you've been picturing in your head — all fair game. Sometimes the best direction comes from the stuff you almost didn't mention.
