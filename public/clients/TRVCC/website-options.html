The short version

A separate piece · either option

The raffle tickets

Separate phasePriced once scoped

Wisconsin treats raffles as regulated gambling, and selling tickets online comes with specific rules and a state raffle license. One thing worth knowing up front: Wisconsin doesn't allow raffle tickets to be sold online through an automated checkout — so even though Zeffy (and platforms like it) advertise a raffle feature, a Wisconsin club can't legally use it that way. The state requires tickets to be handled face-to-face. It's still doable — other Wisconsin clubs run online raffles using a manual, hand-completed process that follows the rules — but that takes a custom approach, which is exactly why I'd treat it as its own phase once the license is sorted, rather than baking it into the first build. That keeps your launch simple and the raffle done right.

Worth a look

What's Zeffy?

It's a fundraising and payment platform built only for nonprofits. It handles donations, store sales, memberships, and event tickets — and because it's funded by optional tips from buyers, the nonprofit pays zero fees. Over 100,000 nonprofits use it.

In plain terms: when you sell a $25 hat or a $125 banquet ticket, you keep the full amount — not $25 minus 2.9% + 30¢ the way you would on Stripe, PayPal, or Shopify. Those processing fees are covered by an optional tip the buyer can add (or skip) at checkout.

I've been watching their walkthrough, and honestly — after seeing it, this looks like it could check a lot of boxes for things you may need, may eventually need, or didn't even know you needed. But I could be wrong; this isn't my organization. You'll know better than me what fits how the club actually runs, so I'd love for you to take a look and tell me what you think.
