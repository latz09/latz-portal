03 Why This Site Matters

Right now, someone in Appleton is searching "stained glass class near me" and finding nothing for The Glass Room. Someone in the Fox Valley is planning a bachelorette party and doesn't know you exist. That changes the day this site goes live.

But this isn't just about being findable. It's about what happens after they find you. Without a signup system, every class booking is a DM, an email thread, a manual confirmation. Sam is tracking seats in her head. Payments are scattered. People who would have signed up at 11pm on a Tuesday don't — because there's no way to.

The signup system doesn't just look professional. It gives Sam and Derek hours back every single week — hours that go into running the café, teaching classes, and making the art that people are coming to see. The waitlist captures revenue that would otherwise walk away. The reminder emails get people back through the door for next month's design. This isn't a brochure — it's an operations platform.

Ways to Adjust Scope

The build above is the full version of what you described. If you want to tighten scope at launch, here are a few items you can defer or simplify — each one can be added later without rebuilding anything.

Defer dedicated private event landing pages
Instead of separate /bachelorette, /birthday, and /corporate pages, use a single private events page with an event-type dropdown on the inquiry form. You'd still capture every lead — you'd just lose the individual SEO rankings per event type. Easy to add the dedicated pages later once you know which event types get the most traction.
saves ~$1,200
Defer open studio subscription to Phase 2
The recurring Stripe subscription for open studio hours is a great feature — but it's also something you could launch without and add once you've run a few months of classes and know the demand. Keeps the payment system focused on class signups at launch.
saves ~$800
Swap CMS-editable menu for PDF upload
Instead of a fully CMS-managed menu with individual items, specials flagging, and seasonal toggling — display a styled PDF that Sam uploads when the menu changes. Still looks good. Just less interactive. The CMS version can be built later if you outgrow the PDF.
saves ~$400
